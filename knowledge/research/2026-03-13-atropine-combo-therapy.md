# 2026-03-13 Atropine and Combination Therapy Research Update

## Key Findings:

*   **Combination Therapies Enhance Efficacy**: Latest research emphasizes atropine's role in combination therapies with optical treatments (e.g., orthokeratology, aspherical lenslet spectacles like Essilor Stellest, HOYA MiYOSMART, or MiSight 1-day lenses). These combinations show enhanced efficacy compared to monotherapy, especially for concentrations like 0.01% and 0.025%.
*   **Strongest Effects with Orthokeratology**: Atropine 0.01% combined with orthokeratology demonstrates the strongest effects in the initial 6 months, with 2025 data confirming additive benefits with highly aspherical lenslet spectacles.
*   **Other Effective Combinations**:
    *   DIMS spectacles with 0.025% atropine.
    *   Orthokeratology with 0.05% atropine.
    *   MiSight 1-day lenses with 0.05% atropine.
*   **Emerging Therapies**:
    *   Red-light therapy (RLRL) with orthokeratology showed axial length reduction in 80% of the combination group in a small study.
    *   MyopiaX (optic nerve stimulation via app) combined with DIMS spectacles after monotherapy suggests potential synergy.
*   **Low-Dose Atropine Monotherapy**: The phase 3 STAR study (2025) confirmed low-dose atropine (SYD-101 0.01%) reduces annual progression, particularly in fast progressors, with good safety. However, experts predict its future as a combo adjunct due to modest standalone results in other trials.
*   **Regulatory Updates**: MHRA approved Ryjunea® (0.1 mg/ml atropine) in 2025 for UK children. Real-world evidence supports 0.025% for efficacy and titration strategies for high progressors.
*   **Minimizing Side Effects**: Reviews note low-dose atropine (<0.1%) minimizes side effects while controlling progression.
*   **Future Outlook**: Researchers anticipate more 2026 studies on varied concentrations and optical combinations to optimize outcomes.
